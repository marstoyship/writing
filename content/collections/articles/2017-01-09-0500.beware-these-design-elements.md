When Lullabot's design team interviewed and surveyed their own front-end developers, they got a list of pain points. 

Custom select elements, responsive carousels, fixed overlays, and inconsistent grids all made the list, rated by difficulty on a scale of one to ten. 

The key takeaway: none of these are impossible, but they all come at a cost. Time-constrained projects force tradeoffs, and designers who understand what's expensive to build make better calls about when it's worth it. 

The article introduces the idea of a "whiz-bang budget" — a finite pool of points for enhancement features — as a practical framework for those conversations.
