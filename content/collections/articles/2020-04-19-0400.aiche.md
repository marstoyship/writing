American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE) is the world's leading professional organization for chemical engineers: 60,000+ members, 100+ technical communities, and a website that had grown too vast and inconsistent for either users or the small internal team maintaining it.

> The result was a design system and redesigned site sustainable enough for a two-person development team to maintain and build on.

I led the UX audit from start to finish. That meant interviewing users, technical community leads, and web team members; reviewing past analytics and member surveys; conducting a full design inventory of custom pages, components, and reusable templates; and auditing the existing information architecture for organizational patterns and naming conventions that didn't match how members actually thought about the site.

From there I synthesized findings into a prioritization framework, evaluating each goal by effort required against positive impact, so the team could make focused, defensible decisions about where to invest. I reorganized sectional groupings to reflect both technical communities and broader topical categories that less-familiar members could navigate, added wayfinding tools for high-frequency content and tasks, and ran usability testing with AIChE members to validate the new designs before launch.

The same prioritization framework drove the design system work: categorizing components into primary, secondary, and tertiary groups based on user interaction, organizational investment, and stakeholder impact.
