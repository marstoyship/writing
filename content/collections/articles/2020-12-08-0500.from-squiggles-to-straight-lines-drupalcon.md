_This talk was given at DrupalCon Global 2020._ 

This talk makes the case for sketching as a core UX and strategy tool — not a preliminary step before "real" design, but a legitimate method for aligning teams, communicating intent, and moving projects forward faster. Drawing on examples from large-scale Drupal projects, it walks through sketch-based artifacts and workshop activities used across discovery, design, and handoff: wireframes, site maps, workshop outputs, remote sketching techniques, and more. The core insight is that sketching's low fidelity is a feature, not a bug — it keeps conversations focused on ideas rather than execution, and makes it cheap to be wrong early. Suited for designers and strategists, but equally useful for project managers and anyone who needs to document complexity quickly and get a room full of people on the same page.
