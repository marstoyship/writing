Georgia Public Broadcasting had grown from a single public television station to a statewide source for news, TV, radio, education, and sports. Their website had accumulated hundreds of thousands of pages across multiple sites that users couldn't navigate.

> Users went from "I don't know where to go" to "I love this change. I can see my options." Direct quotes from GPB usability studies.

My work started with rapid research: customer surveys and interviews, competitor analysis, and content and design system inventories and audits. From that I helped establish three measurable goals for the redesign, with improving wayfinding as the anchor. I created rapid prototypes informed by usability tests and content audits, iterating until we stopped hearing "I can't find anything" and started hearing users ask when they could start using the new site.

The content strategy work shaped how GPB's massive, varied content library would be organized and interconnected on the unified platform. I inventoried and audited all of GPB's content, identified the gaps between what Georgia.gov's editorial tools could do and what public broadcasting actually needed, and defined how content types across news, radio, education, and sports would relate to each other.
