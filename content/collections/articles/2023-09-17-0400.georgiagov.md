Georgia's web infrastructure serves nearly 10 million residents across 80+ agencies, all with different identities, priorities, and audiences, and no mandate to centralize. 

> 75 agency websites launched across 12 rollout cycles, averaging 6–7 launches every three weeks. StateScoop State IT Innovation of the Year award.

My role was to figure out what a flexible, shared platform would actually need to do. I interviewed dozens of agency staff, residents, and governmental stakeholders to understand how people interacted with state websites and where the existing platform fell short. 

Because the scale made manual content inventories impossible, I built custom tooling to automate inventories across all agencies, identify usage patterns, surface accessibility and design problems before migration, and generate pre-populated audit checklists for each agency's own staff to work from.

That research and audit work fed directly into the content model: a completely reimagined structure that centralized key information while giving agencies the flexibility to serve their own audiences. 

I also ran paper prototyping sessions with staff across six agencies to validate Layout Builder before configuration was locked in, and designed the simplified editorial controls integrated with the Orchard design system.
