Context is the variable that determines whether a web experience succeeds or fails, and why designing for a single best-case scenario guarantees you'll miss someone. 

This article catalogs the full range of contextual factors at play: device and connection, physical environment, cultural background, emotional state, ability, familiarity, organizational politics, legacy tech, and more. 

The point isn't that everything is equally variable — it's that any one of these factors, in the wrong combination, can turn a well-designed experience into a harmful one. Facebook's algorithmic missteps serve as the cautionary example. 

The article pairs the IA venn diagram of users, content, and context with a re-illustrated infographic mapping how quickly a person's context can shift mid-task, sometimes in under two seconds.
